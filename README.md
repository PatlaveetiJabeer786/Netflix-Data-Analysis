## 🔴 Business Problem  

Netflix manages a **global content library** of movies and TV shows.  
Executives and analysts face challenges:  
- ❌ Understanding **content trends** across regions  
- ❌ Identifying **popular genres and directors**  
- ❌ Tracking **growth in catalog size over time**  
- ❌ Making **data‑driven decisions** for acquisitions and production  

➡️ Without insights, Netflix risks **inefficient investments** and **missed audience demand**.

---

## ⚙️ What I Did  

╔══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╗
║   🧹 Data Cleaning   |   📊 Exploratory Analysis   |   🎨 Visualization ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╝



- 🧹 **Data Cleaning** → Removed duplicates, standardized dates, fixed missing values  
- 📊 **Exploratory Analysis** → Grouped & aggregated content by year, genre, country  
- 🎨 **Visualization** → Built colorful charts (catalog growth, genre distribution, director contributions)  
- 🧠 **Business Framing** → Linked findings to **content strategy decisions**  

---

## 📈 Results  

- ✅ Showed **rapid catalog growth** post‑2015, driven by original productions  
- ✅ Identified **top genres** (Drama, Comedy, Documentaries) dominating the library  
- ✅ Found **regional differences** in content availability (US vs India vs Europe)  
- ✅ Highlighted **key directors** contributing multiple titles to Netflix’s catalog
